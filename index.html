Short Bio

I am a Ph.D candidate in the Agricultural, Food and Resource Economics Department of Michigan State University. My research is focused on risk and insurance, water management, applied econometrics and food safety. I plan to graduate in spring 2022.

My current work involves estimating the effects of biased premium rate-setting and basis risk on U.S. federal crop insurance participation rates. Nonparametric approach, Gaussian-Copula simulation and other sample selection methods are employed in my study, as well as the econometrics. Furthermore, I am also interested in the estimation of irrigation water cost and the effect on water sustainability in the Great Lakes region.
